# Due Diligence Pricing Proposal

Internal discussion note for corporate pricing.

## Objective

Move from casual-customer pricing to a structure that works for corporations ordering due diligences at scale, while protecting margin on financial statements sourced from official registries.

## Current Retail Pricing

| Item | Retail price | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Due diligence | EUR 22.50 + VAT | Standard current price |
| Financial statements add-on (ES/IT only) | EUR 17.50 + VAT | UK, FR, and CH do not offer this |
| Total with financial statements | EUR 40.00 + VAT | When ES or IT financials are included |

## Suggested Corporate Pricing

| Monthly volume | Due diligence | Financial statements add-on (ES/IT) | Total with financials |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 1-49 | EUR 22.50 | EUR 17.50 | EUR 40.00 |
| 50-199 | EUR 19.00 | EUR 16.00 | EUR 35.00 |
| 200-499 | EUR 16.50 | EUR 15.00 | EUR 31.50 |
| 500-999 | EUR 14.50 | EUR 14.50 | EUR 29.00 |
| 1,000+ | From EUR 12.50 | EUR 14.00 floor | From EUR 26.50 |

## Pricing Logic

- Discount the due diligence aggressively, because margin is high and this is the main lever for winning volume.
- Limit discounts on financial statements, because they depend on third-party official registry costs.
- Keep `EUR 14.00` as the floor for financial statements.
- Make larger discounts conditional on monthly volume or a volume commitment.

## Recommended Commercial Structure

| Plan | Commitment | Due diligence | Financial statements add-on |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: |
| Corporate Flex | No commitment, billed monthly | Volume-based | Volume-based |
| Corporate 200 | 200 reports / month | EUR 16.50 | EUR 15.00 |
| Corporate 500 | 500 reports / month | EUR 14.50 | EUR 14.50 |
| Enterprise | 1,000+ / month or annual agreement | Custom from EUR 12.50 | EUR 14.00 floor |

## Positioning for Customers

- Present the standard retail price as the anchor.
- Explain that official financial statements have limited discount room because they are sourced from chambers of commerce or registries.
- Emphasize that the main corporate discount comes from recurring volume on due diligence reports.
- Reserve custom pricing, invoicing terms, and possible SLA discussions for enterprise customers.

## Open Discussion Points

1. Should discounts apply automatically by monthly usage, or only with a formal commitment?
2. Should there be a minimum monthly spend for corporate accounts?
3. Should enterprise pricing be negotiated case by case above 1,000 reports per month?
4. Should annual agreements include additional benefits beyond pricing, such as priority support or onboarding?

## Working Recommendation

For now, the cleanest proposal is:

- Keep retail pricing unchanged.
- Offer visible volume discounts on due diligence.
- Protect the financial statements margin with a `EUR 14.00` floor.
- Use `200/month` and `500/month` as the main negotiation tiers.
